Oxygen Atom Transfer Level of theory: DFT, CAM-B3LYP-D3BJ functional, mixed def2 basis set

For this project, I worked with my graduate-student mentor, Trevor Lohrey, on employing DFT to model the reaction mechanism of a unique reaction he uncovered. The reaction involved a CO or CNPh ligand binding to the terminal oxo ligand (oxygen atom bound to the Rhenium atom in structure 1) and then dissociating as CO2 or OCNPh. Trevor's reactivity research on structure 1 was used for modeling the reaction pathway.

We hypothesized that the oxygen transfer would involve a CO molecule binding to the terminal oxo ligand forming a η2-CO2 ligand (ηx means x amount of atoms are bound to the metal). However, after an exhaustive search, such a transition state could not be found. We posit that the structure is highly unfavorable hence the inability to isolate the transition state.

However, I was able to find a process in which the CO, first bound to the rhenium (3), then migrated and associated with the oxo-ligand forming a η2-CO2 transition state (TS1). The transition state ultimately led to the intermediate depicted in structure 4.

Next, I had to determine if the substitution of η2-CO2 for CO was favorable. Trevor and I determined that modeling both forms of substitution, associative (CO binds first) and dissociative (η2-CO2 is unbound first) would suffice. Both processes were determined to be energetically favorable. However, the associative mechanism was more favorable and thus depicted in the reaction coordinate below.

The mechanism for CNPh was found to follow the same reaction pathway but more favorably than CO. This is in agreement with Trevor's reactivity studies since, unlike the CO pathway, he was not able to isolate intermediate products for the CNPh pathway.
