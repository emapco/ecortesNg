Borane Activation Level of theory: DFT, wB97XD functional, mixed def2 basis set

In this publication, I analyzed the electronic structures of three similar complexes. I also modeled a reaction mechanism by which boron is activated and is allowed to bond to the metal center. Activation in this context refers to a bond that is cleaved that under normal circumstances would not have.

Electronic Structure Research

All three complexes (structures 1-3 shown in the image below) have similar geometry; each one having an oxo ligand (an atom or group of atoms that are bonded to a metal atom) at the apical position. Thus they are expected to have similar electronic structures.

However, 1's Re-O bonding is significantly weaker with a Wiberg bond index (WBI) value of 1.60. WBI is a formalism for calculating bond strengths. Natural bond orbital (NBO) analysis shows that 1's Re-O contains two bonds as opposed to three bonds present in structures 2 and 3. The weakened bond is also reflected in the natural population analysis (NPA) charges which show a more negatively charged and polarize oxo ligand due to it not sharing electrons with the metal center.

The weakening of the Re-O by the addition of the imido group (NPh) is an example of π-loading. π-loading is the incorporation of ligands with strong double or triple bonds that help saturate the metal's d orbitals. Thus, for Re to facilitate the extra electrons from the NPh ligand in 1, it must weaken its Re-O bond.

I was able to identify a canonical MO (molecular orbital) which contains Re-O π-bonding characteristics (HOMO-16) and a MO with Re-O anti-bonding characteristics (HOMO-1) similar to the lone pair shown in the NBO images below. The HOMO-1 also shows π-bonding characteristics between the Re and imido ligand.

From the visualized NBOs below, you can see that structures 2 and 3 have two Re-O π-bonds each (in addition to a sigma bond giving them a total of 3 bonds). However, structure 1 only has one Re-O π-bond and a lone (electron) pair on the oxygen. The lone pair and the π-loaded electronic structure give rise to structure 1's unique reactivity. Structure 1 is a fascinating compound with distinctive chemistry and is the subject of a few publications by my (undergraduate) research group, the John Arnold group.

Borane Activation Reaction Mechanism Research

Trevor Lohrey's reactivity studies were used as a basis for modeling the mechanistic pathway. Trevor was able to isolate crystals for the starting reagent (1), the product (PD), and the intermediate product (IP) following the transition state (TS). From the isolated crystals, he was able to get crystallographic data. The data was used as the starting geometries for the computations. These geometries were then optimized and can be viewed via the diagram below.

Trevor and I hypothesized that an adduct (structure AD in the diagram below) must precede the isolated intermediate product. I ultimately was able to identify the adduct structure.

Next, I had to locate the transition state. Locating transition states is a bit of an art form since no single method will result in a transition state and it takes a bit of creativity to locate one.

One method requires the starting and ending compounds then an algorithm attempts to locate the transition state. Another similar method requires an additional initial transition state structure. I was never able to get useful output out of these algorithms.

Another method entails using your chemical intuition to model the transition state by hand and then run it through a transition state optimizing algorithm.

Yet another method requires you to fix a parameter or parameters (bond length, bond angle, etc.). You then change it by small amounts while optimizing the rest of the structure. This method produces a potential energy surface (PES) that resembles a parabola with a maximum. You then take the maxima structure and optimize it through a transition state optimization algorithm. I was successful with this method a couple of times on other projects.

The last method that I know and ultimately the one that led me to the transition state required me to animate the starting compound's vibrational modes. I then had to identify which modes might lead to a transition state. Once I identified the modes, I utilized a structure along the animated mode and ran it through a transition state optimization algorithm.

Once you identify a transition state, you need to verify that it contains only one imaginary vibrational mode. A single imaginary vibrational mode means that the structure is at a first-order saddle point along the PES. A structure with more than one imaginary vibration mode is a dud.
